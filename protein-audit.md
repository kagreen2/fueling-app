# Protein Guidelines Audit: Fuel Different vs ISSN Position Stands

## ISSN Position Stand References

### 1. Jäger et al. (2017) - "Protein and Exercise" (JISSN)
- **General exercising individuals**: 1.4–2.0 g/kg/day is sufficient for most exercising individuals
- **Higher intakes (>3.0 g/kg/d)**: Novel evidence suggests positive effects on body composition in resistance-trained individuals (promote loss of fat mass)
- **Per-serving**: 0.25 g/kg or 20-40g absolute dose
- **Endurance athletes**: 1.0–1.6 g/kg/day (Campbell et al., 2007)

### 2. Aragon et al. (2017) - "Diets and Body Composition" (JISSN)
- **Hypocaloric periods (fat loss)**: 2.3–3.1 g/kg FFM (fat-free mass) to maximize lean body mass retention
- **Muscle building**: 1.6–2.2 g/kg/day
- **Higher intakes**: "Increasing dietary protein to levels significantly beyond current recommendations for athletic populations may result in improved body composition"

### 3. Delany et al. (2025) - Scoping Review of Consensus Statements
- **Protein intake**: 1.6–2.4 g/kg/day based on training demands

### 4. Colecchia et al. (2025) - Adolescent Athletes
- **Adolescent athletes**: 1.2–2.0 g/kg/day, with emphasis on 1.4–1.8 g/kg/day

## Audit Findings

### Gain Lean Mass (1.3 g/lb = ~2.87 g/kg)
- **ISSN range**: 1.6–2.2 g/kg (Aragon 2017), up to >3.0 g/kg for resistance-trained (Jäger 2017)
- **Assessment**: ABOVE the standard 1.6-2.2 range but within the >3.0 g/kg evidence for resistance-trained. Defensible but aggressive.
- **Recommendation**: Could lower to 1.1-1.2 g/lb (~2.42-2.64 g/kg) to be more aligned with mainstream ISSN. Current value is still safe.

### Recover & Rebuild (1.3 g/lb = ~2.87 g/kg)
- **ISSN range**: Similar to muscle building, higher protein aids recovery
- **Assessment**: Appropriate — recovery periods benefit from higher protein to support tissue repair.

### Lose Body Fat (1.1 g/lb = ~2.42 g/kg)
- **ISSN range**: 2.3–3.1 g/kg FFM during hypocaloric periods (Aragon 2017)
- **Assessment**: WELL ALIGNED. Note ISSN uses g/kg of FFM (fat-free mass), not total body weight. For a 200lb athlete at 15% BF, FFM = 170lb = 77kg. At 2.42 g/kg total BW, that's ~2.85 g/kg FFM — right in the 2.3-3.1 range. Excellent.

### Maintain & Perform (1.1 g/lb = ~2.42 g/kg)
- **ISSN range**: 1.4–2.0 g/kg for general exercising individuals (Jäger 2017)
- **Assessment**: ABOVE the standard range. For competitive athletes with high training loads, this is on the high end but defensible. For general fitness users, this is notably high.
- **Recommendation**: Consider lowering maintenance for general fitness users to 0.8-1.0 g/lb (~1.76-2.2 g/kg).
